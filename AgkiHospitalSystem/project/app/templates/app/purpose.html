Problem

Access to health services in Tanzania is low and inadequate especially in rural areas. A Half of the population remains below the income poverty line of $1.90 per day. With few resources, deteriorated facilities and a stunning shortage of doctors (just three for every 100,000 people) it was recorded that, about 93% of the population were living within 10 km of a health facility ( WHO, 2021).


Solution

Agki Medical Laboratory decides to ameliorate by providing digital mobile dispensary services{Mobile clinic} including medication, clinic and lab testing, using a solar-powered van for patients in rural areas, particularly pregnant women and children under the age of five. To offer quality services at Agki Centre for all time and to design a platform and APP that operates both online and offline to connect patients to nearby registered hospitals, Health Centre, Polyclinics, Pharmacies, Medical Lab, Doctors specialists, qualified doctors and nurses from the street for emergency/first aid, diagnosis, Medications and treatment. This offers a faster, reliable on-time and consistent rehabilitation hence reducing time and cost to patients and deaths possibly due to late attendance


Purpose & Success Reached

Purpose

Easy accesibity of quality health care services in rural areas.

Reduction of both maternal and under five mortality rate

Success

Coordination of both different health facilities and qualified health professional with patients.

Creation of employment to the different healthcare providers

We helped 40,000 customers includes; pregnant women, under five and patient with permanent disabilities
